About Me:

As a curious creature, I have always had a passion for learning and creating. Like a moth to the flame, I am feverishly drawn to anything that piques my curiosity. As a child these passions were first sparked by music, learning any instrument I could get my hands on. This soon evolved into a greater interest in technology which took its initial form in audio engineering. I spent countless hours teaching myself how to make and record the sounds I heard on my favorite records. I am also a team player. Growing up I played in bands and on teams and I even had the opportunity to play football in college. However, my fervor for creating music overshadowed my desire to play football and I retired from the sport after my freshman year. This led me to pursue a degree in Music Business from Belmont University where I graduated in 2008. Like many a music business major I spent my post college days playing in bands and working odd jobs to make ends meet.

In the summer of 2016, my curiosity and creativity were piqued by the possibilities of software development. I soon began researching online and doing tutorials. I quickly discovered that I had a new passion. I wanted to push myself as far and as quickly as I could into this field. I applied and was accepted to Nashville Software School. During my time there, I fell in love with the whole process of building and creating awesome and scalable apps. Either flying solo or working with a team to solve problems and be creative.

In my free time (when I actually have some) you'll find me coding, reading code, reading books on code, or playing music with friends. I also enjoy binge watching Netflix and playing video games, but I haven't had much time for those lately. While my foray into the field of software development hasn't been a long one, this pilgrimage has been enlightening and enticing. I eagerly await the next opportunity to learn, grow, and flourish.

Project Spotlight: Nugacity

Nugacity is a mobile all things trivia app. Users have three game mode options. Classic Trivia, Jeoparty Mode, or TIL. Trivia mode provides users with random questions from a database of over 40,000. They are given 20 seconds to select the correct answer. If the user answers correctly, their score increases by the amount of time left in milliseconds muliplied by .01. They are given three "Noogs" per game and play until they're "Noogs" are up. Jeoparty mode provides users with random Jeopardy questions from a datbase of nearly 300,000. There is no scoring logic as it is intended to be played with friends users can see the answer by pressing the answer button and play until their brains are full. TIL mode pulls random facts from the Reddit subreddit Today I Learned. Users can view as many as they'd like and save interesting facts to their "Nooguts" which they can view at any time.
